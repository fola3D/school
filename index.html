The Great Migration is the high number of Black Americans moving from the south to the north due to Racism , harsh segregationist laws , and low economic opportunities.

Reconstruction (1865-1877), the era following the Civil War, was the effort to restore Southern states from the Confederacy and newly-freed people into the United States.

After the Reconstruction era, racial inequality was across the South during the 1870s, and the segregationist policies called Jim Crow Affected Black Southerners Negatively.

Black Southerners were still forced to make their living working the land due to Black codes and the sharecropping system, which offered little economic opportunity. And while the Ku Klux Klan had been officially disbanded in 1869, the KKK continued underground after that, and intimidation and violence of Black southerners were not uncommon actions in the South.

All of this led to millions of Black Southerners to move to the north where states are more pro-black Americans.

Jim Crow Laws

type:cause

Jim Crow laws were a collection of state and city laws that were focused on taking the rights of African Americans.

The roots of Jim Crow laws began as early as 1865, immediately after the 13th Amendment was passed. Black codes were city and state laws that said when, where and how Black Americans could work, and for how much compensation.

The legal system was unfair for Black citizens due to former Confederate soldiers being police , judges, etc; making it almost impossible for African Americans to win court cases and ensuring they were submitted to Black codes.

Black Codes

Type: cause

Black codes were laws made to decrease the freedom of African Americans and to make sure they are still a cheap labor source after slavery was stopped during the Civil War. Under black codes, many states required Black people to sign yearly labor contracts. If they didn't sign, they risked being arrested and forced into labor. Outrage over black codes helped undermine support for President Andrew Johnson and the Republican Party.

Sharecropping System

Type: cause

Sharecropping is a type of farming in which families rent small plots of land from a landowner in return for a portion of their crop, to be given to the landowner at the end of each year. With the southern economy in disarray after the abolition of slavery and the devastation of the Civil War, sharecropping enabled landowners to establish a labor force, while giving poor whites and freed Black people a means of subsistence.

The Great Migration Begins

type:effect

When World War I broke out in Europe , industrialized urban areas in the North, Midwest and West faced a shortage of industrial laborers. With war production becoming high , recruiters told Black Americans to come north, to the dismay of white Southerners.

By the end of 1919, some scholars estimate that 1 million Black people had left the South, usually traveling by train, boat or bus; a smaller number had automobiles or even horse-drawn carts. In the decade between 1910 and 1920, the Black population of major Northern cities grew by large percentages, including New York City by 66%, Chicago by 148% , Philadelphia by 500% and Detroit by a whopping 611%.

Many new arrivals found jobs in factories, slaughterhouses and foundries, where working conditions were sometimes dangerous. Female migrants had a harder time finding work, due to heated competition for domestic labor positions. Aside from competition for employment, there was also competition for living space in increasingly crowded cities. While segregation was not legalized in the North (as it was in the South), racism and prejudice were still common.

The Aftermath

type : effect

After The Supreme Court declared racially based housing ordinances unconstitutional in 1917, some residential neighborhoods enacted covenants requiring white property owners to agree not to sell to Black people; these were legal until the Court stopped them in 1948.

Rising rents in segregated areas, plus an increase of KKK activity after 1915, worsened Black and white relations across the country. The summer of 1919 began the greatest period of interracial conflict in U.S. history at that time, including a wave of race riots.

One of these was The Chicago Race Riot of 1919 which lasted for 13 days and left 38 people dead, 537 injured and 1,000 Black families without homes.

Due to problems with Housing , Many Black residents ended up creating their own cities within big cities. The most prominent example was Harlem in New York City that housed about 200,000 Black people.

After moving to the northern states, African Americans were inspired to be creative in different ways. The Great Migration resulted in the Harlem Renaissance, which was also fueled by immigrants from the Caribbean, and the Chicago Black Renaissance.
